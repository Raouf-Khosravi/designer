Assembly Environment

Overview: These assemblies highlight my ability to manage multi-part relationships, ensure motion compatibility, and verify fit and clearance in mechanical systems. From precise hinge mechanisms to gear trains and fasteners, I’ve built assemblies with real-world engineering considerations in mind.

Mechanical Hinge Assembly – Functional Fit & Pin Connection

• Tools Used: Mate, Insert Component, Concentric/Parallel Constraints

• Overview: A multi-part hinge assembly designed to demonstrate proper mechanical fit using pin-and-barrel connections. All degrees of freedom were considered, ensuring realistic rotation around the hinge axis.

• Applications: Door or panel hinges, sheet metal connections.

Toolbox Assembly – Sheet Metal Box with Multiple Components

• Tools Used:Insert Parts, Mate, Exploded View (optional)

• Overview:A multi-part toolbox created with individual panels, handles, and top knobs. Focus was on accurate alignment, part interference checks, and functional closures.

• Applications:Sheet metal enclosures, custom boxes, product design.

Compound Gearbox Assembly – Multi-Gear Transmission System

• Tools Used:Gear Mate, Axis Alignment, Motion Readiness

• Overview:This project simulates a compound gear train consisting of worm gears, spur gears, and bevel gears. Each gear was aligned and mated for proper motion path simulation.

• Applications:Transmission design, motion control systems.

Screw & Nut Assembly – Threaded Fit Demonstration

• Tools Used:Helix Cut, Mate (Concentric + Coincident)

• Overview:A simple but precise assembly of a machine screw and nut, including internal and external threads. Designed to demonstrate thread engagement and rotational fit.

• Applications:Fasteners, product detailing, hardware simulation.
