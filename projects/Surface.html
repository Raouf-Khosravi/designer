Surface Modeling Environment

Overview:These projects reflect my ability to design organic, smooth, and curvature-continuous surfaces using advanced surface modeling tools. I’ve created ergonomic, aesthetically refined models suitable for consumer products and mold-ready prototypes.

Spoon – Curvature-Continuous Freeform Design

• Tools Used:Surface Loft, Surface Fill, Knit Surface, Thicken

• Overview:A freeform spoon modeled using smooth surface blending to achieve ergonomic and aesthetic quality. The design prioritizes curvature continuity (C2) for a seamless visual and functional experience.

• Applications:Industrial product design, ergonomic cutlery, consumer goods.

Fork – Thin-Wall Surface with Tapered Tines

• Tools Used:Surface Loft, Boundary Surface, Mirror, Thicken

• Overview:A detailed fork design created through surface modeling to simulate real-world cutlery with sharp, symmetric tines. Designed for aesthetic precision and manufacturability in metal or plastic.

• Applications:Product styling, mold design, prototyping.

Knife – Hybrid Surface-Solid Ergonomic Handle

• Tools Used:Surface Extrude, Surface Loft, Surface Knit, Combine with Solid

• Overview:This kitchen knife model integrates both surface and solid modeling to achieve a smooth ergonomic handle and a sharp, functional blade. The handle was shaped based on human grip studies to ensure comfort and usability.

• Applications:Consumer product design, kitchenware, ergonomic tools.
